CIROH and IJC Collaborative Projects

A series of projects have been awarded to the CIROH researchers by the International Joint Commission (IJC) to prepare inputs (downscaled, bias-corrected, and disaggregated climate change projections) and develop tools (hydrological models) required to support water resources analysis in the Souris River Watershed.

Partners

Project 2. Preparing the Inputs and Tools Required to Support the Climate Change Modeling Chain for Application to Water Resources Analysis in the Souris River Watershed

The Project 2 is undertaken to produce historical downscaled, bias-corrected, and disaggregated set of atmospheric inputs for the hydrological models. The project also intends to investigate the reliability of atmospheric inputs produced by two different downscaling and bias-correction techniques to generate evaluated and proven dataset for the entire Souris River Basin (SRB).

Goals

  • Develop future downscaled, bias corrected, and disaggregated climate variables
    • Daily surface timeseries for six variables (e.g., Precipitation, temperature, specific humidity, incoming longwave radiation, incoming shortwave radiation, and barometric pressure)
    • 3-hourly timestep for all variables from 2015 to 2100
    • SSP2-4.5, SSP3-7.0, and SSP5-8.5 for a subset of 12 General Circulation Models (GCMs) with a rationale for selection of GCM subset from the CMIP6 GCMs
    • Workflow for future extension to the North American domain and to cover other transboundary river basins
  • Provide a description of the limitations of the final dataset along with a plan to make further improvements

Research Activities

1. Bias correction and downscaling

The project used a state-of-the-art downscaling and bias-correction method known as Semi-Parametric Quantile Mapping (SPQM, Rajulapati and Papalexiou, 2023) to produce historical and future climate change projections data for the entire North America as shown in the map below. Using the SPQM method, the downscaled and bias-corrected daily precipitation, minimum and maximum temperature were produced, and the data were stored for 15 regions (see the map) for the convenience.

2. Temporal disaggregation

The key part of the project is to generate 3-hourly disaggregated climate data for six variables (listed under the project goals) using MetSim (Bennett et al., 2020) method based on the downscaled and bias-corrected daily climate variables (precipitation and minimum/maximum temperature). A total of 12 GCMs was selected to produce the downscaled, bias corrected and disaggregated data. Each model includes a complete set of three climate change scenarios (ssp245, ssp370, ssp585) to be considered, all utilizing consistent realization/forcing. Although downscaled and bias-corrected daily climate variables are made available for the North America (1-15 regions on the map), the 3-hourly disaggregated data are produced for four regions (regions 7, 8, 10, and 11) covering the Souris River Basin.

3. Data Storage, Dissemination, and Public Repository

The 3-hourly disaggregated climate data for six variables are stored on the Graham server of Compute Canada (graham.computecanada.ca). The data will be made available upon request
